"Sri Lanka Explores Digital Taxation Amid Rapid Technological Advancements"

2024/05/02

As digitalization transforms the global economy and reshapes traditional business models, Sri Lanka is exploring new avenues for taxation to capture revenue from digital transactions and online platforms. The government is considering various options for digital taxation, including the implementation of a digital services tax (DST), taxation of e-commerce transactions, and establishment of a framework for taxing multinational tech giants operating in the country. However, the adoption of digital taxation measures faces challenges related to tax administration, enforcement, and international coordination, as well as concerns over potential negative impacts on innovation, investment, and consumer welfare. As Sri Lanka navigates the complexities of digital taxation, stakeholders are calling for a balanced approach that promotes revenue generation while supporting digital innovation and inclusive growth agendas.
