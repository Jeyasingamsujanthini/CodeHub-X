"Tax Policy Uncertainty Casts Shadow Over Sri Lanka's Economic Outlook"

2024/05/02

Uncertainty surrounding Sri Lanka's tax policy direction is weighing heavily on investor sentiment and economic prospects, as businesses grapple with the lack of clarity and predictability in the country's tax regime. The government's recent flip-flops on tax rates, exemptions, and incentives have created a sense of instability and confusion among taxpayers, hindering long-term investment planning and capital allocation decisions. Moreover, concerns over the country's fiscal sustainability and debt management have led to calls for greater transparency and consistency in tax policy formulation and implementation. As Sri Lanka seeks to rebuild its economy and attract foreign investment in the post-pandemic era, addressing tax policy uncertainty is paramount to restoring confidence and fostering sustainable growth.
